Neuralink has developed a new BMI (Brain Machine Interface) that is designed to be minimally intrusive. Historically the threads that have been inserted into the brain have been stiff, and rigid which helps in penetrating the brain, but the problem with this is that it can elicit negative immune responses that limit the usability, and longevity of the devices. Neuralink uses thin, flexible threads that are less destructive, and also far more capable of reading data than previous BMI’s have been. The problem with this is that it becomes a lot more difficult to actually connect the threads to the brain. To solve this problem Neuralink has created a robot that can perform the surgery with incredible accuracy, while at the same time being capable of automatically inserting 6 threads per minute. During Neuralink’s study the surgery took 45 minutes on average (Musk). Neuralink designed the BMI in a way that won’t require another surgery if you want to update your hardwear. There is a small device that would sit behind your ear, which is the only thing that needs to be replaced when you want to upgrade your equipment. They plan on conducting human trials in 2020 on paralysis patients in an attempt to help them restore motor functions (Cold Fusion). Technologically I think that Neuralink is capable of making this technology commercially available (especially when looking at Elon Musk’s track record), but there are other problems with this technology.
